Understanding COVID-19

Help to make the world a better place


COVID-19 is spread by close person-to-person contact and over long distances through respiratory droplets from speaking, coughing or sneezing.



Symptoms may appear 2 to 14 days after exposure to the virus that causes COVID-19. A person does not have to have symptoms to spread the virus.



COVID-19 can only be diagnosed with a laboratory test done in person or at the home.
